Chefs Life

About me

My name is Kirito and I'm traditionally trained in Japanese cuisine. I have worked in street kitchens all the way up to three Michelin star restaurants, but now I'm trying to share my stories and recipes with the world while learning as much as I can about food around the world.

My journey started in Japan when I was 7 years old and helped my mom make Yakitori, ever since that moment I have been in love with food and devoted my life to it. I saved up money for culinary school by working in street kitchens in Japan until I was 22, then I went to Paris to attend Le Cordon Bleu culinary school. Here I made friends for life and a network that would help me land my first job as an official cook in the streets of Paris.

I had been cooking all my life but I still felt this was the real start of my culinary journey. I worked with some of the best chefs in Paris and even had the chance to work with Guy Savoy, the same chef who was Gordon Ramsay's mentor. After working in Paris for five years I took a one year "vacation" working as a private chef on a yacht traveling the world. It was on this journey I realized it was more to life than working 15 hour days in a kitchen and is the reason for why I'm here on this blog sharing my recipes with the world.
